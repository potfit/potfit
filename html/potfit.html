User Guide

Authors

potfit was originally developed by Peter Brommer during his diploma thesis and doctoral thesis work.

The development of potfit is continued by Peter Brommer as part of his PhD thesis work. This work was funded through the DFG, Collaborative Research Centre (SFB) 382 "Simulation physikalischer Prozesse auf Hochleistungsrechnern", Projekt C14 "Molekulardynamik von Quasikristallen". Work is continued through Collaborative Research Centre 716 "Dynamische Simulation von Systemen mit groﬂen Teilchenzahlen", project B.1 "Molekulardynamik groﬂer Systeme mit weit reichenden Wechselwirkungen".

You are free to use potfit within the limits set by the licence, but I request you acknowledge its use by citing the relevant publications on this program.

potfit is available for download.

It is strongly recommended that all potfit users sign up to the potfit-announce mailing list to keep informed about all patches and bug fixes.

There is also the potfit-users mailing list. There you can discuss with other users and the potfit team, ask questions, report bugs, request enhancements and submit patches or other contributions. You have to subscribe to post to the list.

For extensive information on the potfit package, its background, theoretical foundation and results obtained with it, have a look at the doctoral thesis of Peter Brommer (in english language). It is published online right here
